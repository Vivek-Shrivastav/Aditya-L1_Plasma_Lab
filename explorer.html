ASPEX Payload

Aditya Solar wind Particle EXperiment (ASPEX) consists of two sub-systems: SWIS and STEPS.

SWIS (Solar Wind Ion Spectrometer) is a low energy ionic spectrometer that measures ions in the energy range of 100 eV to 20 keV.

STEPS (Supra-Thermal & Energetic Particle Spectrometer) measures high energy ions in the range of 20 keV to 5 MeV.

ASPEX Technical Specs

  • Ion Range 100 eV - 5 MeV
  • Angular Res ±45° × ±45°
  • Energy Res ~7% (ΔE/E)
  • Sampling Rate 2 - 128 sec

PAPA Payload

Plasma Analyser Package for Aditya (PAPA) is designed to understand the solar wind composition and its energy distribution.

It consists of two sensors: SWEEP (Solar Wind Electron Energy Probe) and SWIC (Solar Wind Ion Composition).

PAPA Technical Specs

  • Electron Range 10 eV - 3 keV
  • Ion Mass Range 1 - 40 amu
  • Field of View ±45° Fan-beam
  • Power Cons. ~7.5 Watts

Scientific Methodology

The analysis of in-situ plasma data at L1 follows established ISSI and international space science protocols for Level-2 CDF processing.

01

Calibration & Decommutation

Raw telemetry from PRADAN is decommutated into physical counts. Calibration curves for MCP efficiency and geometric factors are applied to derive particle flux.

02

Moment Calculation

Numerical integration of the Velocity Distribution Function (VDF) yields the primary plasma moments: Density (n), Bulk Velocity (V), and Temperature (T).

03

Frame Transformation

Magnetic field vectors from MAG are rotated from the instrument frame into geophysical frames like GSE (Geocentric Solar Ecliptic) or GSM.

04

MVA Analysis

Minimum Variance Analysis is performed on B-field discontinuities to identify directional boundaries, shocks, and rotational structures in the solar wind.

L1 Physics

The Sun-Earth L1 Lagrange point is a point of unstable equilibrium 1.5 million kilometers from Earth.

  • Distance from Earth ~1.5 million km
  • Distance from Sun ~148.5 million km
  • Station Keeping Halo/Lissajous Orbit
  • Warning Window 30 - 60 minutes
