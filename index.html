MISSION CONTEXT

Why the L1 Lagrange Point?

The Sun–Earth L1 point is ~1.5 million km from Earth — a gravitational saddle where a spacecraft can orbit the Sun synchronously with Earth, maintaining an unobstructed view of the Sun and continuous solar wind sampling.

⏱ 45–60 Min Early Warning

Solar wind at L1 reaches Earth ~45–60 minutes later. This advance warning enables space weather alerts before CMEs and high-speed streams arrive and compress Earth's magnetosphere.

🔭 Unobstructed Sun View

L1 allows continuous Sun-facing observation. Remote sensing instruments (VELC, SUIT) always see the Sun, while in-situ instruments (ASPEX, PAPA, MAG) sample the undisturbed solar wind arriving from the Sun.

🌊 Pristine Solar Wind

Unlike measurements inside Earth's magnetosphere, L1 samples the solar wind before it interacts with the geomagnetic field — the "upstream" boundary conditions that drive all space weather at Earth.

📡 Continuous Monitoring

Aditya-L1's halo orbit maintains near-continuous line-of-sight to both the Sun and Earth simultaneously — essential for the uninterrupted data relay that makes real-time space weather monitoring possible.

INSTRUMENTS

In-Situ Plasma Payloads

Aditya-L1 carries seven instruments. Three are remote sensing (Sun-facing). Four are in-situ: they physically sample the solar wind plasma at L1. This observatory focuses on the in-situ suite.

ASPEX — Aditya Solar Wind Particle EXperiment

Full nameAditya Solar Wind Particle EXperiment
Sub-instrumentsSWIS + STEPS
Science goalSolar wind composition, suprathermal ions, energetic particle origin and acceleration
Data productsIon spectra, flux, moments, anisotropy

ASPEX is the composite in-situ particle suite. It comprises two very different instruments — SWIS for thermal solar wind and STEPS for suprathermal/energetic particles — covering an enormous energy range that spans from the bulk solar wind to galactic cosmic rays.

Science Objectives

ASPEX addresses three foundational questions in heliophysics:


OriginWhere do suprathermal particles come from?
AccelerationHow does the solar wind accelerate particles to MeV energies?
TransportHow do energetic particles propagate through the heliosphere?
CompositionHow does the alpha-to-proton ratio vary with solar wind type?

SWIS — Solar Wind Ion Spectrometer

SpeciesProtons (H⁺), Alpha particles (He²⁺)
Energy range100 eV – 20 keV
AnalyserElectrostatic analyser + imaging detector
Angular coverage~360° in ecliptic plane
Data productsIon spectra, bulk moments (n, V, T), α/p ratio

SWIS uses a toroidal electrostatic analyser that selects ions by energy-per-charge. The full 360° angular coverage allows determination of the complete velocity distribution function, from which all plasma moments are derived.

Temperature TProton thermal energy in eV (1 eV ≈ 11,600 K)
α/p ratioHe²⁺/H⁺ number density ratio (~3–4% typical)
VDFFull velocity distribution function f(v)

STEPS bridges the gap between the thermal solar wind (SWIS) and galactic cosmic rays. It is crucial for understanding particle acceleration at interplanetary shocks and solar energetic particle (SEP) events.

PAPA — Plasma Analyser Package for Aditya

SpeciesSolar wind ions and electrons
Science focusVelocity distribution functions (VDFs), plasma moments
Key productsn, V vector, temperature tensor P, electron heat flux Q
Unique valueElectron measurements complement SWIS ion data

PAPA is essential for characterising the full plasma state. While SWIS measures ions, PAPA provides the electron side of the distribution — necessary for computing the full pressure tensor and understanding heat flux, wave-particle interactions, and electron-driven instabilities.

MAG — Magnetometer

Measurement3-component vector IMF: Bx, By, Bz
LocationDeployed boom away from spacecraft body
CoordinatesGSE (Geocentric Solar Ecliptic) — verify in file headers
Key productsB vector, |B|, clock angle, cone angle, PSD

The magnetometer measures the interplanetary magnetic field (IMF) vector. The Bz component (north-south in GSM) is the single most important parameter for space weather — southward Bz drives dayside reconnection and geomagnetic storms via the Dungey cycle.

Parker angleExpected ~45° at 1 AU for 400 km/s wind

DATA TUTORIAL

What Does Each Measurement Mean?

Before visualizing data, understand what you're looking at. Each parameter has a physical meaning, a typical range, and diagnostic value. Hover any card to see formula details.

n Proton Number Density

Number of protons per cubic centimetre at L1. This is the zeroth moment of the velocity distribution function — the integral of f(v) over all velocities.

n = ∫ f(v) d³v ≈ 4π ∫ f(v) v² dv

Quiet SW: 3–10 cm⁻³ · CIR compression: 15–30 · CME sheath: >30

A sudden density jump (×2–4) combined with velocity and field jumps = interplanetary shock. Low density + smooth field rotation = CME flux rope interior.

V Solar Wind Bulk Speed

First moment of the VDF — the mean flow velocity. Encodes the solar source region: slow wind (300–450 km/s) from streamer belt, fast wind (550–800 km/s) from open coronal holes.

V = (1/n) ∫ v f(v) d³v

Slow wind: 300–450 km/s · Fast wind: 500–800 km/s · CME: up to 1000+ km/s

Speed profiles reveal stream interaction regions (CIRs) where fast wind overtakes slow wind, forming compressed plasma and shocks.

T Proton Temperature

Second moment of the VDF — the thermal energy per particle. Related to the width of the velocity distribution. Hot, fast wind expands nearly adiabatically.

T = m/(3kₙ n) ∫ (v−V)² f(v) d³v

Slow cold wind: 3–8 eV · Fast hot wind: 15–30 eV · CME ejecta: very cold, T < expected

Anomalously low temperature for a given speed is a key CME ejecta signature (cold + fast + smooth B = flux rope).

|B| IMF Total Magnitude

Magnitude of the interplanetary magnetic field vector. Expected to decrease as ~1/r from the Sun. Enhancements signal compressed regions (sheath, CIR) or reconnecting structures.

|B| = √(Bx² + By² + Bz²)

Quiet: 3–8 nT · CIR/sheath: 10–25 nT · Strong CME: 30–60+ nT

|B| alone cannot tell you about geoeffectiveness — you need the Bz component and clock angle.

Bz IMF Northward Component

The north-south component of the IMF in GSM coordinates. The most critical space weather parameter. Southward Bz (negative) enables dayside magnetic reconnection via the Dungey cycle.

Reconnection rate ∝ V_sw · B_T · sin²(θ/2)

Northward (+): minimal coupling · Weakly south (−1 to −5 nT): mild · Strongly south (<−10 nT): storm

A southward Bz of −10 nT sustained for 3+ hours typically drives a geomagnetic storm (Dst < −50 nT).

β Plasma Beta

Ratio of thermal pressure to magnetic pressure. Determines whether the plasma is magnetically or thermally dominated, setting the character of waves and instabilities.

β = n k_B T / (B²/2μ₀) = P_thermal / P_magnetic

β ≪ 1: flux rope interior · β ~ 1: typical solar wind · β ≫ 1: CIR compressed region

β < 1: Alfvén and magnetosonic waves dominate dynamics. β > 1: kinetic effects, instabilities, enhanced particle-wave coupling.

V_A Alfvén Speed

Speed at which magnetic tension perturbations propagate along field lines. Sets the characteristic velocity scale for magnetohydrodynamic (MHD) wave propagation.

V_A = B / √(μ₀ ρ) = B / √(μ₀ n m_p)

Typical at 1 AU: 40–80 km/s · Solar wind is super-Alfvénic: M_A = V/V_A ~ 6–10

The solar wind must transition from sub-Alfvénic near the Sun to super-Alfvénic (M_A >1) — this transition defines the Alfvén critical surface, which Aditya-L1 studies remotely via VELC.

E(f) Power Spectral Density

Distribution of magnetic field fluctuation power across frequencies. Reveals the turbulent energy cascade from MHD scales down to ion kinetic scales. Key diagnostic from Paschmann & Daly Ch. 1.

E(f) ∝ f^α · Kolmogorov: α = −5/3 · IK: α = −3/2

Inertial range: −5/3 · Kinetic range (below ion scale): −7/3 to −4

A spectral index shallower than −5/3 indicates energy injection (shock, CME). Steeper than −2 indicates sub-ion scale kinetic dissipation.

ANALYSIS METHODS · ISSI SR-001

Scientific Analysis Toolkit

Methods follow Analysis Methods for Multi-Spacecraft Data (Paschmann & Daly, ISSI SR-001). Each card links to the interactive tool in the Analysis Lab.

CH. 1 · SPECTRAL ANALYSIS

FFT · Welch PSD · Morlet Wavelet

Transform time-series data from the time domain into the frequency domain. Reveal turbulent energy cascades, wave modes, and spectral indices. Welch's averaged periodogram reduces variance. Morlet wavelets handle non-stationary signals.

Eriksson 1998 · Paschmann & Daly Ch. 1

CH. 5–6 · PLASMA MOMENTS

Density · Velocity · Temperature · Pressure Tensor

Compute the moments of the velocity distribution function: n (zeroth), V (first), T (second). These reduce the full VDF to a small set of macroscopic fluid quantities that govern bulk plasma behavior.

Paschmann & Daly Ch. 5–6
Moments Calculator →
CH. 8 · MVA

Minimum Variance Analysis of the Magnetic Field

Find the coordinate system where B-field variance is minimised — the normal direction to a boundary crossing, shock front, or magnetic cloud. The ratio λ_int/λ_min > 2 indicates a reliable normal.

Sonnerup & Scheible 1998 · Ch. 8
Open MVA Tool →
CH. 10 · DISCONTINUITIES

Rankine-Hugoniot Shock Relations

Apply conservation of mass, momentum, and energy across a shock to determine shock parameters: compression ratio, Mach number, shock angle θ_Bn. Distinguish between shocks, tangential discontinuities, and rotational discontinuities.

Schwartz 1998 · Ch. 10

Resampling · Filtering · Gap Handling

Practical pre-processing: resample multi-instrument data to a common cadence, handle data gaps via interpolation, apply anti-aliasing filters, verify Nyquist sampling requirements before spectral analysis.

VDF · Pitch-Angle Distributions · Anisotropy

DATA ACCESS

How to Get Aditya-L1 Data

All Aditya-L1 science data is available through PRADAN (PRoducts and Archives Data ANalysis) at ISSDC. No long proprietary period — data is released on a rolling basis after processing.

1

Register

Create a free account at pradan.issdc.gov.in. Required for data download.

2

Browse datasets

Navigate Aditya-L1 → instrument → Level-1 or Level-2 → year → date → files.

3

Download CDF files

Data is in CDF (Common Data Format). Use cdflib (Python) or NASA's CDF library to read. Session timeout ~30 min — use bulk download for large datasets.

4

Or upload here

Drop any CDF file into the Data Visualizer for instant plotting — no coding needed.

⚠️ Scientific cautions: Do NOT assume cadence values — check the CDF DEPEND_0 attribute. Do NOT assume variable names — inspect headers first. Calibration parameters may be updated; check payload user guides for each download.
