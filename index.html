01 — Mission Context

Why the L1 Lagrange Point?

The Sun–Earth L1 point sits 1.5 million km sunward — a gravitational saddle where Aditya-L1 orbits the Sun synchronously with Earth, sampling pristine solar wind before it reaches us.

⏱

45–60 Min Early Warning

Solar wind detected at L1 reaches Earth ~50 minutes later. This window enables geomagnetic storm alerts before CMEs compress the magnetosphere and disrupt satellites and power grids.

🔭

Unobstructed Sun View

L1 maintains continuous Sun-facing geometry. Remote instruments (VELC, SUIT) observe the corona uninterrupted, while in-situ instruments sample the solar wind arriving from upstream.

🌊

Pristine Solar Wind

Unlike near-Earth measurements, L1 sits upstream of Earth's bow shock — sampling the solar wind before it interacts with the magnetosphere. These are the true boundary conditions for space weather.

📡

Continuous Monitoring

Aditya-L1's halo orbit maintains near-continuous Earth visibility. Combined with the uninterrupted Sun view, this enables real-time data relay and 24/7 space weather monitoring.

02 — Instrumentation

In-Situ Plasma Payloads

Aditya-L1 carries seven instruments. Four are in-situ — they physically sample the plasma at L1. This lab focuses exclusively on those four.

ASPEX — Aditya Solar Wind Particle EXperiment

The composite in-situ particle suite. ASPEX combines SWIS (thermal solar wind, 100 eV–20 keV) with STEPS (suprathermal and energetic particles, 20 keV–6 MeV/n) — covering six decades in energy to trace particles from the bulk wind through to galactic cosmic rays.

Sub-instrumentsSWIS + STEPS
Science goalComposition, origin, and acceleration of solar wind and energetic particles
Key questionsWhere do suprathermal particles come from? How does the Sun accelerate them to MeV?
Data productsIon spectra, bulk moments, directional flux, anisotropy, pitch-angle distributions

SWIS — Solar Wind Ion Spectrometer

Measures the thermal solar wind using a toroidal electrostatic analyser that selects ions by energy-per-charge. Full 360° ecliptic coverage enables complete velocity distribution functions for protons and alpha particles.

SpeciesProtons (H⁺), Alpha particles (He²⁺)
Energy range~100 eV – 20 keV
Analyser typeToroidal electrostatic analyser + imaging detector
Angular coverage~360° in the ecliptic plane
Data productsIon spectra, n, V, T, α/p ratio, full VDF
⚠ Verify actual variable names, cadence, and energy channel definitions from the official SWIS payload user guide. Do not assume values.

STEPS — SupraThermal & Energetic Particle Spectrometer

Bridges the gap between thermal solar wind and galactic cosmic rays using six solid-state detectors pointing in different directions. Critical for studying particle acceleration at interplanetary shocks and solar energetic particle (SEP) events.

Energy range~20 keV – 6 MeV/nucleon
TechniqueSolid-state detectors (SSDs)
Detectors6 directional, including Sun-radial, Parker spiral, Earth-pointing
SpeciesElectrons, protons, heavier ions
Data productsDirectional flux, energy spectra, anisotropy, pitch-angle distributions
⚠ Exact look directions must be confirmed from SPICE kernels or the STEPS user guide. The nominal directions above may not reflect as-flown geometry.

PAPA — Plasma Analyser Package for Aditya

Measures both solar wind ions and electrons, providing full velocity distribution functions and derived moments. The electron measurements are especially valuable — PAPA sees the electron strahl (field-aligned beam) that traces magnetic connectivity back to the Sun.

SpeciesSolar wind ions and electrons
Science focusVDFs, pressure tensor, electron heat flux, temperature anisotropy
Key productsn, V vector, T tensor, heat flux Q, electron strahl
Unique valueElectron strahl traces magnetic connection to Sun; T_perp/T_par tests instability thresholds

MAG — Magnetometer

Measures the three-component vector IMF on a dedicated boom away from the spacecraft body to minimise contamination. IMF Bz is the single most critical space weather parameter — southward Bz drives dayside reconnection via the Dungey cycle and controls energy input to the magnetosphere.

Measurement3-component vector B: Bx, By, Bz
LocationDeployed boom — minimises spacecraft field contamination
CoordinatesGSE (Geocentric Solar Ecliptic) — always verify in file headers
Key productsB vector, |B|, clock angle θ, cone angle φ, PSD of fluctuations
IMF Bz < −10 nT sustained for ≥3 hours typically drives a geomagnetic storm (Dst < −50 nT). The clock angle θ = arctan2(By, Bz) — 180° is maximally geoeffective.
03 — Data Tutorial

What Does Each Measurement Mean?

Before reading a plot, understand the physics behind each quantity — its moment in the VDF, its typical range, and what it tells you about the Sun's state.

n
SWIS · PAPA
Proton Number Density
Zeroth moment of the velocity distribution function — the integral of f(v) over all velocities. Sets the mass density of the solar wind.
n = ∫ f(v) d³v ≈ 4π ∫ f(v) v² dv
Quiet: 3–10 cm⁻³ CIR: 15–30 cm⁻³ CME sheath: >30 cm⁻³
V
SWIS
Solar Wind Bulk Speed
First moment — the mean flow velocity. Directly encodes the solar source region: slow wind from the coronal streamer belt, fast wind from open coronal holes.
V = (1/n) ∫ v f(v) d³v
Slow: 300–450 km/s Fast: 500–800 km/s CME: >800 km/s
T
SWIS · PAPA
Proton Temperature
Second moment — thermal energy per particle. Hot fast wind expands adiabatically. Anomalously cold + fast is a key CME ejecta signature (flux rope interior).
T = m/(3k_B n) ∫ (v−V)² f(v) d³v
Slow cold: 3–8 eV Fast hot: 15–30 eV CME ejecta: <expected T
|B|
MAG
IMF Magnitude
Total interplanetary magnetic field strength. Drops as ~1/r from the Sun. Enhancements signal compressed regions (CIR, CME sheath) or structured flux ropes.
|B| = √(Bx² + By² + Bz²)
Quiet: 3–8 nT CIR/sheath: 10–25 nT Strong CME: >30 nT
Bz
MAG · GSE
IMF North-South Component
The most critical space weather parameter. Southward Bz enables dayside magnetic reconnection via the Dungey cycle. Reconnection rate ∝ V_sw · B_T · sin²(θ/2).
θ (clock angle) = arctan2(By, Bz)
North +: minimal coupling South −5 nT: moderate South −10 nT: storm
β
Derived
Plasma Beta
Ratio of thermal to magnetic pressure. Determines whether plasma dynamics are governed by waves (β < 1) or kinetic effects (β > 1). Sets the character of instabilities.
β = nk_BT / (B²/2μ₀) = P_thermal/P_magnetic
β≪1: flux rope interior β~1: typical SW β≫1: CIR compressed
V_A
Derived
Alfvén Speed
Speed at which magnetic tension perturbations propagate along field lines. The solar wind is super-Alfvénic (M_A ≈ 6–10) at 1 AU — this is why a bow shock forms at Earth.
V_A = B / √(μ₀ρ) = B / √(μ₀ n m_p)
Typical 1 AU: 40–80 km/s M_A = V/V_A ~ 6–10
E(f)
MAG · PSD
Power Spectral Density
Distribution of magnetic fluctuation power across frequencies. Reveals the turbulent energy cascade. The spectral slope α is the primary turbulence diagnostic.
E(f) ∝ f^α | Kolmogorov: α = −5/3
Inertial: α = −5/3 Kinetic: α < −2.5 Energy injection: α > −1
04 — Analysis Methods · ISSI SR-001

Scientific Toolkit

Methods from Analysis Methods for Multi-Spacecraft Data (Paschmann & Daly, ISSI Scientific Report SR-001). Each technique is implemented in the Analysis Lab.

Chapter 1 · Spectral Analysis

FFT · Welch PSD · Morlet Wavelet

Transform time-series into the frequency domain. Welch's averaged periodogram reduces variance. Morlet wavelets handle non-stationary signals like CME transients. Reveals turbulent cascades, wave modes, spectral indices.

Eriksson (1998) · Paschmann & Daly Ch. 1
Open Spectral Lab
Chapters 5–6 · Plasma Moments

Density · Velocity · Temperature · Pressure Tensor

Compute the moments of the velocity distribution function: n (zeroth), V (first), T (second), P tensor (second). These macroscopic fluid quantities govern bulk plasma behavior.

Paschmann & Daly Ch. 5–6
Moments Calculator
Chapter 8 · MVA

Minimum Variance Analysis

Finds the coordinate system where B-field variance is minimised — the normal direction to a boundary crossing, shock, or magnetic cloud. Quality criterion: λ_int/λ_min > 2 for a reliable normal.

Sonnerup & Scheible (1998) · Ch. 8
MVA Tool
Chapter 10 · Discontinuities

Rankine-Hugoniot Shock Analysis

Apply conservation of mass, momentum, and energy across a shock to determine compression ratio, Mach number, and shock angle θ_Bn. Classify tangential vs rotational discontinuities.

Schwartz (1998) · Ch. 10
Shock Analyser
Chapter 2 · Time Series

Resampling · Filtering · Gap Handling

Pre-process multi-instrument data: resample to a common cadence, apply anti-aliasing filters, handle gaps via interpolation, verify Nyquist requirements before spectral analysis.

Harvey (1998) · Ch. 2
Time Series Tools
Chapter 5 · Distributions

VDF · Pitch-Angle · Anisotropy

Compute pitch-angle distributions relative to the local B field (from STEPS), test fire-hose and mirror instability thresholds from T_perp/T_par, detect beam-like or strahl populations.

Fazakerley (1998) · Ch. 5
Distribution Lab
05 — Data Access

Getting Aditya-L1 Data

All Aditya-L1 science data is freely available through PRADAN at ISSDC on a rolling release basis. No long proprietary period after Level-2 processing.

1

Register at PRADAN

Create a free account at pradan.issdc.gov.in. Required for data download. Use these credentials in GitHub Secrets for automated fetching.

2

Browse datasets

Navigate: Aditya-L1 → instrument → Level-2 → year → date. Level-1 is calibrated; Level-2 is science-ready with derived moments.

3

Download CDF files

Data is in CDF (Common Data Format). Use cdflib (Python) to read. Session timeout ~30 min — use bulk download for large datasets.

4

Or use this lab

Upload any CDF/JSON file directly in the Data Visualizer for instant plots — or set GitHub Secrets for automated 6-hour fetches.

⚠ Do NOT assume cadence values — check the CDF DEPEND_0 attribute. Do NOT assume variable names — inspect headers first. Calibration may be updated; re-check payload user guides with each download.
